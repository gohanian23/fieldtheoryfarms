Why we do this

Mushrooms aren't plants.
They're something else entirely.

We started Field Theory Farms because we couldn't stop thinking about fungi. Not just as food — as organisms. The way mycelium networks underground, sharing nutrients, sending signals, solving problems without a brain or a blueprint — it's one of the most remarkable systems in nature.

Fungi aren't classified as plants or animals. They're their own kingdom, and they've been here for over a billion years. The networks they build predate trees. The intelligence in those networks — distributed, adaptive, relentless — is something worth paying close attention to.

We believe there's a way of growing, connecting, and building that we can learn from them. We started with the most honest expression of that: grow them well and feed people.
"Mycelium is nature's internet. We're just learning to listen."
The networks fungi build underground share nutrients, send chemical signals, and support entire ecosystems. We think that's worth paying attention to — and worth building a farm around.

How we grow

The grow, the harvest, the handoff.

01
Controlled environment
Every grow room is calibrated to the exact humidity, CO₂, and light conditions each species needs. No pesticides. Substrate ingredients available on request. The science is the product.
02
Harvested to order
We don't harvest and hope someone buys it. Chefs book their window, and we time the flush around their kitchen. Market customers get same-morning harvest — picked at 3am, at your table by 9.
03
Direct, always
You're buying from the person who grew it. No distributor, no cold chain gap, no mystery. That transparency is the standard we hold ourselves to — and the reason chefs keep coming back.
