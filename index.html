Why we do this

Mushrooms aren't plants,
they're something else.

We started Field Theory because we got obsessed with fungi. Not just as food, but as organisms. The way mycelium networks underground, sharing nutrients, sending signals, solving problems without anything resembling a brain — it has been doing this for over a billion years.

Fungi aren't classified as plants or animals. They're their own kingdom. The intelligence in those networks — distributed, adaptive, relentless — is something worth paying close attention to.

We believe there's a way of growing, connecting, and building we can learn from them. We started with the most honest thing we could do: grow them well and feed people.

"Mycelium is nature's internet. We're just learning to listen."
— Field Theory, Los Angeles
